    • PROJECT ENERGY

    Energy has always played an important role in defining the state of Texas. But how have other states compared? For our group project, we wanted to answer this question by looking at three key areas across all states: the production of energy, the consumption of energy, and the prices each state paid for that consumption. The US Energy Information Administration has a vast repository of information on these topics which can be found at https://www.eia.gov Information was collected and placed into a sqlite database for use across all charts on this website through using the Flask library in Python. Additional data was provided by using US Census geojson information on borders and boundaries.
    Using Leaflet.js with a choropleth overlay, we see the average price of energy per state in 2019. Lower prices could be found not only in Louisiana but in the Upper Rockies. Higher prices are centered more in the Northeast due to additional consumption of home heating oils during the winter months. Alaska and Hawaii also had high average energy prices due to their location.
